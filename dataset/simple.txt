William_Shakespeare
Shall_I_compare_thee_to_a_summer's_day?
Thou_art_more_lovely_and_more_temperate:
Rough_winds_do_shake_the_darling_buds_of_May,
And_summer's_lease_hath_all_too_short_a_date;
Sometime_too_hot_the_eye_of_heaven_shines,
And_often_is_his_gold_complexion_dimmed,
And_every_fair_from_fair_sometime_declines,
By_chance,_or_nature's_changing_course_untrimmed:
But_thy_eternal_summer_shall_not_fade,
Nor_lose_possession_of_that_fair_thou_ow'st;
Nor_shall_Death_brag_thou_wand'rest_in_his_shade,
When_in_eternal_lines_to_time_thou_grow'st.
So_long_as_men_can_breathe,_or_eyes_can_see,
So_long_lives_this,_and_this_gives_life_to_thee.
